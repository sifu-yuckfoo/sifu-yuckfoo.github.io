About AIG

Institution, Leadership & Philosophy

Akamai Intelligence Group (AIG) is a Hawaii-based, minority-owned research and development laboratory specializing in adversarial resilience, distributed stigmergic coordination architectures, and institutional governance for autonomous multi-agent AI systems. "Akamai" means intelligent in Hawaiian.

AIG operates at the intersection of distributed systems engineering, multi-agent governance theory, and contested-edge autonomy. The research program investigates whether distributed autonomous cognitive systems can remain governable, resilient, and operationally stable under adversarial conditions -- using governance-first institutional design rather than centralized control or benevolence assumptions.

Christopher Ramos is the founder, sole owner, and principal investigator of Akamai Intelligence Group LLC. His research program focuses on governance-first distributed cognition -- designing multi-agent AI architectures that remain operationally stable and recoverable under adversarial conditions without central oversight dependencies.

The research originated from an unconventional but empirically grounded methodology: Culinary Operations Analysis. Drawing on deep professional expertise as an Executive Chef and high-volume operations system designer, Ramos adapts the organizational architecture of the classic Kitchen Brigade into formal distributed coordination models. In high-volume culinary operations, complex tasks execute asynchronously across specialized stations without top-down micromanagement. The environment itself -- through tickets, physical staging, and standardized SOPs -- functions as a stigmergic state bus. Failure at one station does not halt the line. The system absorbs the shock, redistributes load, and maintains continuity. This battle-tested operational model forms the architectural intuition underlying AIG's distributed governance framework.

This methodology produced the foundational architectural insight of AIG's research program: resilience in complex systems traces not to the quality of individual components but to the governance topology connecting them. Compartmentalization, bounded authority, role specialization, and fault isolation are not safety features added to a system -- they are the structural conditions that make safety possible. That insight reframes alignment from a property of isolated models to a property of system architecture.

Ramos leads all aspects of AIG's research, engineering, and business development. The current portfolio includes six provisional patents, five published research papers spanning adversarial multi-agent governance, biomimetic coordination topology, and human-AI trust dynamics, and an active proposal to the DARPA DICE program for Phase I SBIR funding.

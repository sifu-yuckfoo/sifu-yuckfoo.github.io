About

Akamai Intelligence Group — Principal Researcher & Founder

Christopher Ramos is the founder and principal researcher of Akamai Intelligence Group (AIG), a Hawaii-based, minority-owned R&D laboratory specializing in adversarial resilience, governed multi-agent AI coordination, and distributed synthetic cognition.

AIG's governance-first architecture is not the product of a traditional computer science background. It is the product of decades of high-pressure, systems-level operational thinking — forged in professional kitchens and refined through self-directed technical research.

From the Kitchen to the Architecture

Christopher spent years as an executive chef — a role that demands the same operational discipline that defines AIG's research. A professional kitchen at scale is a real-time distributed system: multiple specialized agents executing parallel tasks under strict latency constraints, resource contention, cascading failure modes, and a human command authority who must maintain situational awareness without micromanaging every station.

The parallels are not metaphorical — they are structural. Station hierarchy maps directly to role-bounded agent architecture. Mise en place is provenance and pre-computation. Fire coordination is sparse, typed inter-agent signaling. The expediter is the governance control plane. A broken chain of command in a kitchen, like a broken authority boundary in a multi-agent system, produces cascading failures that are invisible until they are catastrophic.

That operational intuition — built across years of running high-throughput, high-consequence human systems — is what shaped AIG's core thesis: governance is not a constraint on performance. It is the mechanism of performance.

About AIG

Akamai Intelligence Group LLC is a Hawaii-based, minority-owned (full Filipino) research and development laboratory. AIG's primary research focus is adversarially resilient, human-commanded governance architecture for heterogeneous multi-agent AI systems operating in contested, long-duration, degraded environments.

AIG's work is aligned with DARPA's Distributed Intelligence in Contested Environments (DICE) program and adjacent to the Lightweight Universal Codec (LUC) special notice. All research is conducted as an independent laboratory without government affiliation, endorsement, or program-performer status.
